Resources

  • The Google Places API allows you to query for place information on a variety of categories, such as: establishments, prominent points of interest, geographic locations, and more. You can search for places either by proximity or a text string. A Place Search returns a list of places along with summary information about each place; additional information is available via a Place Details query. You can see the full Google Places API documentation here: https://developers.google.com/places/documentation/.

    -SEE THE DOCS! https://developers.google.com/places/documentation/index

  • Important google places API is a PURE REST/JSON API i.e. it only uses browser key not ios/android keys .. so use server (or browser) key with no referrals (can use from anywhere)

Sample POI Data for deCarta App

Grand Central Station
42-43rd St & Park Ave/Lexington Ave,
Beautiful world’s largest railway station (76 acres) 100 by 650 foot high ceilings train shed of glass and steel made in 1931 where millions travel through weekly with hanging 13 foot wingspan iron eagles tall skyscrapers.


  • Excellent-quality retail shops and restaurants have taken over the mezzanine and lower levels.
  • West mezzanine has the Michael Jordan’s-The Steak House, a gorgeous Art Deco space that allows you to dine within view of the sky ceiling as well as the gorgeously restored The Campbell Apartment, which serves cocktails. Off the main concourse at street level, there’s a nice mix of specialty shops and national retailers, as well as the truly grand Grand Central Market for gourmet foods.
  • The shuttle passage with the New York Transit Museum Store, houses city transit-related exhibitions and a terrific gift shop that’s worth a look for transit buffs.
  • The lower concourse houses a stellar food court and the famous Oyster Bar & Restaurant.
  • The whispering corners are fun, stand face to the wall ( in the corner ) get someone to stand in the opposite corner and whisper, you can hear perfectly. Star constellations are painted on the ceiling,
  • The main, Southern entrance, has sculptures of Mercury, Hercules, and Minerva above the world’s largest Tiffany clock.
  • The main concourse, inside, is mostly marble and truly elegant. The four white sections of the timepiece above the Grand Central Terminal information booth are actually made of pure opal – and have been valued between $10 to $20 million!

Times Square
5th Avenue
The and distracting, there are Overcrowded with camera-wielding tourists in day with overpriced places to eat, and plenty of souvenir shops and street vendors selling cheap NYC memorabilia. Its special charm are the bright neon lights that create a world famous speial atmosphere that you have to see at least once in NYC and are copied in much larger ways in Tokyo, Beijing, etc.

Central Park

Chinatown
New York has 3 Chinatowns. One in Manhattan, Queens, and Brooklyn.
Little China in heart of NYC with all the little stores and all of the great Chinese restaurants.
The streets are filled with knockoff bags and watches and plastic toys made in China.

Enjoy true Chinese cuisine in all its variety by eating at a Dim Sum Restaurant - “Har gow” ( shrimp dumplings), “siew mai” (pork dumplings), “lor bak go” (turnip cake), “char siew bao” (steamed roast pork buns), “dahn taht” (egg custard, much like creme brulee but baked pastry) and “jook” (congee/porridge with variety of meat and scallions).

For one week in February Manhattan Chinatown celebrates the Chinese New Year where there are parades and festivals galore no matter the temperature. There is a museum of Chinese Americans, beautiful temples, Chinese ornaments, fountains, furniture, Chinese teas, and Chinese herbs. Early in the morning in Christopher Park and some other open spaces you will find people doing Tai Chi. It is free to join them. On Mott street you will find many accupressurist charging $10 for 10 minutes. Accupressure is like a medical massage or sports massage/physical therapy.

Rockefeller Center
47th-51st Streets and between 5th and 6th Avenues.

Rockefeller Center represents the best in New York past grandeur seen in movies, with its lights and decorations, shops, ice to skate in winter and the Radio City Music Hall. The show in Radio city is only once a day and is over-rated for a 1 day trip, but worth it if you have more time.

Empire State Building
Address: 350 5th Avenue
Phone: (212) 947-1360
Website: http://www.esbnyc.com/

Going up to the observatory on the 86th floor, you can go out of the door and enjoy the view from the terrace. of Empire State Building is a must-do item for tourists early in your trip - you can enjoy really splendid view of NYC. The spectacular night view of the city is very charming. The entrance to the building is on 5th Ave. The ticket office is on the 2nd floor - the 86th floor is $20, and a second elevator goes to 102nd floor for total cost of $35. You are supposed to go up to the 102nd first. Late afternoon lines can be long, but just after opening 8:00 there may be no wait to get to the observation deck. It is open daily 8:00 a.m. to 2:00 a.m. Adults $20, Children 6-12 $14, Seniors $18.
