Indoor Air Quality Top Tips

If you have asthma, your number one enemy is dust mites.

For people with allergies, pollen and dander are a big problem.

These are all microscopic elements you can’t see with the naked eye, and they’re commonly found in air ducts. A good cleaning will remove a lot of them.

HVAC inspection, Cleaning,Air Ducts

Know your HVAC/Air Ducts

  • Know your HVAC/Air Ducts System
  • Pros and cons
  • EPA on Should You Have the Air Ducts in Your Home Cleaned?
  • Symptoms of a Dirty or Toxic HVAC/Air duct system
  • The National Air Duct Cleaners Association recommends getting your air ducts cleaned every two to five years, depending on what part of the country you’re in, how often you use your system, and if anyone in your home has allergies or asthma.
  • US EPA recommends immediate duct cleaning
  • You suspect mold exists in the air ducts, or significant in other parts of the home.
  • There is a rodent or vermin infestation somewhere in the home, or you see insects or rodents entering or exiting ductwork.
  • You can SEE visible “poof” of dust released from the supply registers when air is exiting the vents.

Professional Air Duct Cleaning tips

  • Really good air duct cleaning

A good cleaner is going to use a negative air machine (vacuum)

  • A strong blower can be used if your HVAC furnace has an access to blow air out from heat output. You block all other vents

  • Pro Duct cleaners use a rotary brush cabling system - sort of a roto-rooter for your air ducts. The brush goes through the ducts and knocks the debris loose. Coupled with a vacuum at the vent outlet the loose dust is vacuumed away.
