Near Real Time Stats with GA

While GA doesn’t have true real time analytics which is hard for nearly every tool, even million dollar self built for your own site and supported by an entire staff of experts.

However use a simple trick to get almost up-to-the-minute statistics without shelling out cash for a premium service. Google updates your statistics on an hourly basis. To get that data, all you need to do is click on the date selector calendar in your
Analytics report, and select the current date as the end date. Of course, this data isn’t completely up-to-date, but
up to the hour is likely close enough to take fast and intelligent action when your content goes viral.

Normally, when you click on the date drop-down, you’ll notice that the blue bar indicating the selected date only
goes up to yesterday. To get it to show real-time data, select the first date in your preferred range, and then
click the latest date that isn’t grayed out. And voila! You have almost real time tracking.

CLEAN DATA: IP Exclusion for More Accurate Data

  • IP exclusion (so you don’t inadvertently measure yourself or your web team)
  • Just before launch, your traffic stats will be distorted by huge spikes as your content/devs keep touching and testing your site. You might be excited into first few weeks of launch till you find all of the traffic came from the same IP maybe the same browser yours :)

Just use the IP exclusion feature in Google Analytics by

  • Click on the “Edit” button for your site on the main profile page
  • On the “Profile Settings” page, scroll down the page until you see the box named “Filters Applied to Profile” (below
    goals).
  • Click on the “Add Filter” link, and you’ll be taken to the “Create New Filter” page.
  • Just stick in your IP address (or range of IP addresses) so that it is excluded from GA
  • Click “Save Changes” and you’re all set.
