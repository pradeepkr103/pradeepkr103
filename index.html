Saving Lives Wins First Place!

Saving Lives Wins at Juniper Hackathon Feb 20, 2016

The SVHacks hackathon was held at Juniper Feb 20, 2016

Project - Effective Altruism $5 can save a life

  • Over 250 hackers attended the SV Hacks-Hackathon hosted by the IIT Bombay Heritage Foundation, From India Abroad Article dated 3/18/2016 page A23, talked with Saket Gadia, President, San Francisco Bay Area Chapter, IIT Bombay Heritage Foundation..

    The winning hack was one titled ‘Saving Lives’ — a plat- form to make donations to one’s favorite charities and track the impact of the contributions in terms of ‘impacted lives.’ Gadia explained that the platform also provided a social aspect where you could see what one’s friends are doing and how much impact they are having to make ‘saving lives a habit’.

Technology Stack

  • App Prototyped Integrating Uber donations and Pinterest to promote the best projects to Save Lives.
Technology Logo About
Uber Donations when using Uber and UberEats
Pinterest Pinterest to promote most effective charities

Team

Pradeep Jain

Jains are believers in Ahimsa or non-violence. But even better it is to be proactive and actually save lives.
Donating $5 to the right charity can pay for enough number of mosquito screens which can save one life.

Pradeep did the product implementation strategy and focus on features and on what to showcase.

  • Implemented a native Android app that showed the giving log and commitments and pledges made.
  • Integration with Braintree (partially completed)

Ajay Jain

  • Ajay did an Ionic prototype for IOS and Android for Saving Lives.

  • Ajay has won Global hackathons including Car/Telematics of the Auto Council, AT&T Global Cleantech hackathon, and Tata Telecommunications Education hackathon, as well as First place in Tech Disrupt 2013.

  • He is currently a MIT student and HackMIT organizer and a leader promoting hackathons at 10 universities.

Sameer Jain

Behavioral designer, product manager, and growth strategist focused on supporting health and happiness through digital tech.
“I have long been intrigued by Effective Altruism, but after reading through the compelling logical arguments advanced by the EA movement, felt that there was a missed opportunity to connect people to the impact they’re making - to give them the “warm fuzzies” that are a primary driver behind altruistic action. GiveWell founder Holden Karnofsky talks about Excited Altruism, but when browsing the Giving Pledge’s site, I just couldn’t find myself feeling a level of excitement that matched the level of impact I’d actually be having. SavingLives is an attempt to bridge that gap.””
