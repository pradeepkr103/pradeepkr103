Book Buying and Sales & Textbooks

Sell, Donate or Recycle

You may want to make a rule only to sell a book for a dollar or more, and then donate the rest. Also, be aware that scouting sites can be unreliable, so check the actual buyer sites as well to confirm their rates. For example, TextbookRush tends to accept quite a few titles that others don’t, but often for only $0.10. Think about whether this is worth the effort.

Many used book buyback services require a minimum dollar amount or number of books to process your shipment. See the full list of condition guidelines and rules for all services via their websites.

Trolling for Used Books of Higher Value

  • Some troll thrift shops with laser scanners that tell them the going rate for a book in the Amazon Marketplace. These scanners can even be equipped with apps so that traders can create a custom algorithm, instantly notifying them of whether a book is worth buying to re-sell.
  • http://www.slate.com/articles/arts/culturebox/2010/10/confessions_of_a_usedbook_salesman.2.html

Making the Money on Shipping

  • Amazon sellers are given a “shipping credit” - often buyers see the base price, but the shipping credit may have far more profit than the book itself. If it costs you $1 by USPS Media mail and you get $3 credit, but may only get $2 on a $1.5 book that you acquired in bulk. So you make barely 0,5 on book, but $2 on shipping.

Only offer best condition ones on big sites

The condition of the used books would have to be factored into the price. Just put yourself in the buyer’s shoes, you would not want to pay good money for a torn book with missing pages. As a rule of thumb, I would not go there to list my books unless they are decently maintained, in light of the keen competition over at Amazon, from the very minted books to the real scrappy ones.

Tips for Selling goods

  1. Be honest about the condition of the book. If there are damaged corners or missing pages, say so. If someone has written in or highlighted huge portions of the book, disclose that. If selling service or buyer finds differently, it may result in either returned or disposed.

  2. Package your goods carefully.If it was in excellent condition, but gets damaged in shipping, it will be the same as shipping damaged goods. You’’re responsible for getting the book there in one piece, so take the time to wrap them well.

Alternatives to sell books, Creative Alternatives

Textbooks.com

Despite its name, Textbooks.com does not only accept textbooks, so don’t discount it, even for paperbacks. It could be the service that takes several of your books that no one else will. Textbooks.com may even offer you a superior bid here and there.

Minimum: $10
Payment: PayPal or check
Shipping: Free via UPS

Textbook Buyer

The website has a reputation associated to buying used textbooks for the past 10 years. The company has featured in the Wall Street Journal, among some established publications in the country. It is much heralded for its fair pricing policy, free shipping and prompt payment. In addition to used textbooks, it is also willing to accept new textbooks at special prices.

Cash4books

Instead of creating a marketplace to make your used books available to potential buyers, they will buy them direct from you.
However, they don’t just buy any book.

Still, the kind of books they are willing to take on are still a lot. In order to find out if your books are in the desired list, you need to supply the IBSN number to the website.

They’ll then give you an offer for the book(s). If you like the offer and want to sell your book(s) for that price, they’ll send you a prepaid shipping label that you can use to ship the book(s) to them.

Once they receive the book(s), they’ll inspect it and if approved, they’ll send ou the payment via PayPal or by check, depending on your preference.

Powell’s Books

Like Cash4books - but more stringent on book condition.

If you keep your books in pretty minted condition, you can expect to get a couple of dollars more by selling them to this website.

http://www.powells.com/sellonline/
Powell’s, a chain of independent bookstores in Portland, Ore., often pays quite well for used books online, but it is a stickler for condition and does not return rejected books. Powell’s does not accept ex-library books, books with rips or taped pages, or books with notes or highlighting. It would be worthwhile to read the full condition guidelines. If you take the virtual store credit, you’ll get a better price, but Powell’s will also pay promptly through PayPal.

Minimum: $5 or 3 books
Payment: PayPal or store credit
Shipping: Free via USPS

Sell Back Your Book

Sell Back Your Book will take care of the shipping fees as they buy your books.

They do bulk purchase from libraries.

SellBackBooks

The site will buy it directly from you. Simply enter the ISBN number and see how much they pay for it. If you like the price, they will send you a prepaid shipping label which you can use to send the book in.
Once they receive the book, they pay you fast with a direct deposit to your bank account.

RentBooks

Again, very similar to other sites where you simply enter the ISBN number, or the author or book name, get a price, print a shipping label, send the book in, and get paid.
Choose if you’d rather get a check in the mail or money sent directly to your PayPal account. It just can’t get any easier than this.

Blue Rectangle

  • This buys directly from buyers but offers data entry alternativs to ISBN ie UPC code as well.
    You will receive payment only after passing their inspection

CKY Books

buys books direct from you is CKY Books. Their range is more diverse and they range from fictional to non-fictional, religious books to textbooks, self-help books to travel books, and much more.
It will also absorb the shipping cost and you either get paid by PayPal or check.

KnetBooks.com

You can buy, sell and rent all kinds of books here.
